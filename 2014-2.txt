Optimism surrounds Occidental Petroleum, as it gets ready to report its second quarter results on Thursday, July 31, 2014. Analysts are expecting the company to book a profit of $1.75 a share, up from $1.58 a year ago.

Over the past month, the consensus estimate has dipped from $1.76, but it's still up from the consensus estimate of $1.73 three months ago. Analysts are projecting earnings of $7.31 per share for the fiscal year. After being $6.12 billion a year ago, analysts project revenue to drop 1% year-over-year to $6.04 billion for the quarter. For the year, revenue is projected to come in at $25.12 billion.

Revenue dropped year-over-year in the first quarter, breaking the three-quarter streak of revenue growth.

The company has posted increasing profit for three quarters in a row. In the first quarter, net income rose 5% while it rose more than fourfold in the fourth quarter and 15% in the third quarter.

The majority of analysts (60%) rate Occidental Petroleum as a buy. This compares favorably to the analyst ratings of 10 similar companies, which average 50% buys.

Occidental Petroleum is an international organization with subsidiaries operating in the oil and gas, chemical, and midstream markets. Exxon Mobil, also in the oil and gas - integrated industry, will report earnings on Thursday, July 31, 2014. Analysts are expecting earnings of $1.91 per share for Exxon Mobil, up 23% from last year's earnings of $1.55 per share. Other companies in the oil and gas - integrated industry with upcoming earnings release dates include: Eni S.p.A, Chevron and Marathon Oil.
